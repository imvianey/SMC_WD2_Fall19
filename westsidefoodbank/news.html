Cuts to SNAP

December 7, 2019

Last week, the federal government published a final rule enacting significant cuts to the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), impacting basic food assistance for an estimated 700,000 Californians struggling to find work. This is a cut of historic proportions, the largest since the 1996 “Welfare Reform” Act. Contrary to the USDA’s rationale, in reality, this rule does not promote self-sufficiency, wellbeing, or economic mobility. Rather it targets our community members – like veterans and the formerly incarcerated – with the greatest barriers to work by making these individuals hungrier and poorer. It also places an impossible burden on food banks and other emergency food providers to fill in where our Federal Government falls short.
